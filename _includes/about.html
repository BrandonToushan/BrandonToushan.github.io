About me

I am a recent Queen's University grad with a passion for developing data-based solutions for education, healthcare and wellness-based applications. My particular focus is in transforming raw datasets into beautiful, easy to understand visuals and metrics in order to make data-based problem-solving more accesible to those from non-technical backgrounds. My educational background and experience is largely rooted in physics, mathematics, and statistics with the bulk of my past projects and knowledge stemming from these fields. In this capacity, I have extensive experience developing mathematical models and machine-learning algorithms for various scientific applications, most notably the SuperCOOP project; a recreation of the 1972 Nobel Prize-winning BCS theory experiment. On the more human side of things, I have considerable experience both working in, and managing teams at the KidsAbility Centre for Child Development, a wonderful NGO located at the intersection of wellness, healthcare, education and accesibility.
